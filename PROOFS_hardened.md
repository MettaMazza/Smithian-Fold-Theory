# Smithian Fold Theory — definitions and proofs (paper standard)

## Definitions

**D1 (the One).** The One, written 1, is unity: the whole.

**D2 (position / part of the One).** A *position* is an exact rational p/q with 0 < p/q ≤ 1, a part of the One. The value 1 is *unison* (the whole returned to itself). Zero is not a position.

**D3 (casting out the One).** For a positive rational m, `cast_out(m)` removes whole Ones while the magnitude exceeds one whole: it returns the unique r ∈ (0,1] with m = j + r for some nonnegative integer j (j = ⌈m⌉ − 1). Each removal acts on a magnitude strictly greater than 1, so every intermediate and the result are strictly positive.

**D4 (the fold).** For a position r, `fold(r) = cast_out(r + r)`. Since r ∈ (0,1], r+r ∈ (0,2]; if r+r ≤ 1 then fold(r) = r+r, otherwise fold(r) = r+r − 1 ∈ (0,1]. The boundary r = ½ gives fold(½) = 1 (unison).

**D5 (fold factor m).** The m-fold replaces doubling by r ↦ cast_out(m·r) for an integer m ≥ 2.

**D6 (bit of a fold).** The *bit* at a position r is 1 if r + r ≥ 1, else 0 (whether the fold casts out a One).

**D7 (ratio / relation).** The relation between two positions a, b is the proportion a/b. Unison is the relation 1.

**D8 (separation).** For positions a ≠ b, `separation(a,b)` is the smaller of the two arc-parts between them, in (0,½]; for a = b it is unison (1). Concretely, with hi = max(a,b), lo = min(a,b): one = hi − lo ∈ (0,1), other = 1 − one ∈ (0,1), separation = min(one, other).

**D9 (antipode).** `antipode(p) = cast_out(p + ½)`, the position a half-One from p.

## Theorems and proofs

**R1 (Q4) — the One unfolds to exactly 2^k distinct positions in k folds.**
*Proof.* By D6 each fold yields a bit in {0,1}, so k folds yield a bit-string in {0,1}^k, of which there are 2^k. The map from a length-k bit-string b₁…b_k to the position Σ_{i=1}^{k} bᵢ·2^(−i) is the base-two value of 0.b₁…b_k; on strings of length k this map is injective, because two distinct strings differ at some first index i, forcing values differing by at least 2^(−k) before the lower-order parts (each < 2^(−i)) can compensate. Hence the 2^k strings fix 2^k distinct dyadic positions to depth k. (Injectivity verified exhaustively for k ≤ 8: 255 nonzero strings → 255 distinct parts, no collision.) ∎

**R2 (Q5) — the fold reveals one part per fold; the revealed parts reassemble the position exactly.**
*Proof.* Write r = 0.b₁b₂b₃… in base two. Then r + r = b₁.b₂b₃…, so the bit cast out is b₁ and fold(r) = 0.b₂b₃… : the fold drops the leading part and shifts the rest up. By induction the bit read at fold i is bᵢ. For a position with terminating expansion of length k, Σ_{i=1}^{k} bᵢ·2^(−i) = r exactly. (Verified: 11/16 = 0.1011 → bits 1,0,1,1 → ½+⅛+1/16 = 11/16.) ∎

**R3 (Q6) — count and measure are one quantity; count × measure = the One.**
*Proof.* A position fixed to k folds lies in the dyadic arc [m·2^(−k), (m+1)·2^(−k)) for some m; there are exactly 2^k such arcs (R1), each of width 2^(−k) (a part of the One). Thus count N = 2^k and measure = 2^(−k) = 1/N, whence N · measure = 2^k · 2^(−k) = 1. Count and measure are the single quantity k (the fold depth) read two ways; the information to name one arc is exactly k bits. ∎

**R4 (Q7) — the even division of the whole is fold-fixed.**
*Proof.* Let M be even and consider the M-even division E_M = {i/M : i = 1,…,M}. For each i, fold(i/M) = cast_out(2i/M) = (2i mod M)/M when 2i > M, else 2i/M; in either case the image is a multiple of 2/M, i.e. a member of E_{M/2} = {j/(M/2) : j = 1,…,M/2}. The map i ↦ fold(i/M) is exactly two-to-one onto E_{M/2}: indices i and i + M/2 have images differing by cast_out of M/M = 1, hence equal, and no other coincidences occur. Therefore E_M folds onto E_{M/2} with each part attained exactly twice; the image is again an even division (equal gaps 1/(M/2)). The evenly-divided whole is returned to an evenly-divided whole. (Verified for M = 8, 16, 32, 100: images = M/2, each reached exactly twice, onto E_{M/2}.) ∎

**R5 (Q8) — self-perception repels: under independent folding the separation multiplies by the fold factor, for separations up to one quarter of the One.**
*Proof.* Let two positions have separation s. Under the m-fold each position maps by r ↦ cast_out(m·r), so the arc between them maps to cast_out(m·(arc)); for the *short-way* separation this gives m·s **provided m·s ≤ ½**, since then m·s is itself the short way. Hence for s ≤ 1/(2m) the separation multiplies exactly by m ≥ 2 and grows; the diagonal s → unison is a repelling state. (For larger s the short-way wrap applies — e.g. doubling s = 3/10 gives short-way 2/5, not 3/5 — so the clean multiplier holds on s ≤ 1/(2m).) (Verified: s = 1/1000, 1/100, 1/17 → ratio 2; wrap case s = 3/10 → 2/5.) ∎

**R6 (Q9) — relative views telescope and commute.**
*Proof.* The view of b from a's frame is the proportion b/a (D7). Composing the view of c from b with the view of b from a multiplies the proportions: (c/b)·(b/a) = c/a, the view of c from a. Multiplication of positive rationals is associative and commutative, so relative position telescopes and the order of composition is immaterial. ∎

**R7 (Q10) — the holding threshold is the ratio (m−1)/m; no π appears.**
*Proof.* Two coupled positions with separation s evolve, per fold, by the repel (R5: factor m) followed by the pull, which keeps the fraction (1 − g) of the separation: s ↦ m·(1 − g)·s. The separation fails to grow exactly when m·(1 − g) ≤ 1, i.e. 1 − g ≤ 1/m, i.e. g ≥ (m − 1)/m. So holding begins at g* = (m − 1)/m — the half-One for m = 2, two-thirds for m = 3, and so on. At g* the per-fold factor m·(1 − g*) = 1 exactly. No π enters; π arose earlier only through a sine-and-radian rendering, which the permitted language does not use. (Verified: m·(1 − g*) = 1 exactly for m = 2,3,4.) ∎

**R8 (Q11) — domain closure: the system is closed under its own operation.**
*Proof.* The objects generated from the One are: positions, even divisions, bit-strings, fold-depths, tuples of positions, and proportions. The fold maps each into the domain: a position to a position (D4, range (0,1]); an even division to an even division (R4); a bit-string to its left shift (R2); a depth k to k+1; a tuple coordinatewise to a tuple; a proportion to a proportion (the fold acts on the positions, ratios persist). No application of the fold leaves the domain; the system is closed under its own operation. ∎

**R9 (Q12) — opposition of relations: a relation times its opposite is the One.**
*Proof.* The opposite of a relation r is its reciprocal 1/r (D7). Their composition is the product r · (1/r) = 1. The multiplicative identity, the One, is the balance state: balance is the One, not zero. Holds for every positive rational r. ∎

**R10 (Q13) — opposition of positions: a position and its antipode are a half-One apart.**
*Proof.* By D9 the antipode of p is q = cast_out(p + ½). The two arc-parts between p and q are ½ and 1 − ½ = ½; the short way (D8) is ½. So separation(p, antipode(p)) = ½, the maximal separation, for every position p. ∎

**RB1 (E1) — interlocking cycles return at the least common multiple of their periods.**
*Proof.* A position with odd denominator is purely periodic under the fold (its orbit is finite and contains the start, since doubling is a bijection on the residues coprime to the odd denominator), with some period L. Two such positions a, b with periods L_a, L_b, folded in lock-step, return jointly precisely when both have returned, i.e. after a number of folds divisible by both L_a and L_b; the least such is lcm(L_a, L_b). So the joint orbit — the beat — has period lcm(L_a, L_b). (Verified: periods 3,4 → combined 12; 2,4 → 4; 3,6 → 6.) ∎

**RB2 (E2) — a power-of-two lattice folds into the single region of the One.**
*Proof.* Every position i/2^k is dyadic with terminating expansion of length ≤ k; by R2 the fold shifts its bits, and after at most k folds all bits are exhausted and the position is the One, where it remains. A lattice of such positions therefore folds into the single region containing the One. (This is an exact edge; odd-denominator lattices instead stay periodic and spread — R4, RB1.) ∎

**RB3 (E5) — the separation-modulation is periodic with the beat period.**
*Proof.* For two purely-periodic positions a, b with periods L_a, L_b, the joint state (fold^n(a), fold^n(b)) is periodic with period L = lcm(L_a, L_b) (RB1). The separation at step n is a function of the joint state alone, so the separation sequence is periodic with period L: the modulation between unison and the half-One repeats exactly every beat period. (Verified periodic for the tested pairs.) ∎

## Consistency and the content of the results

**C1 (consistency of the definitions, by the rational model).** Interpret each primitive in ℚ ∩ (0, 1] with ordinary arithmetic: `cast_out` sends a magnitude m ∈ (0, 2] (more generally any positive rational) to the unique r ∈ (0, 1] with m = j + r, j a nonnegative integer; `fold(r) = cast_out(r + r)`; `antipode(p) = cast_out(p + ½)`; `separation` is the smaller of the two arc-parts, equal to the half-One when the parts are equal. Each is a total, single-valued function on its stated domain — verified on a wide sample including the wrap case p + ½ > 1 (156 positions) and the exact half-One case. Since every definition is realised as a total function in the rationals, any contradiction derivable from D1–D9 would be a contradiction in ℚ; hence the definitions are **consistent relative to the rationals**. The machine development, run in exact `Fraction` arithmetic, is a witness to this model. ∎

**R11 (Q14) — the fold identifies a position with its antipode; the antipodal pair is the fold's fiber.** For every position p, fold(p) = fold(antipode(p)): writing antipode(p) = cast_out(p + ½), we have 2·antipode(p) = cast_out(2p + 1), and casting out the extra One leaves cast_out(2p) = fold(p). Conversely, if fold(q) = fold(p) then 2q ≡ 2p (mod the One), so q = p or q = p + ½ cast out, i.e. q ∈ {p, antipode(p)}. Thus every image has exactly two preimages, and they are an antipodal pair. (Verified: every tested fiber has exactly two points forming an antipodal pair; antipode is an involution.) This is the two-to-one structure underlying R4, now identified explicitly: the pairs the fold merges are exactly the antipodal pairs. ∎

**C2 (coherence of opposition).** The system carries two opposition structures, on two types, and they are one principle: *opposites return to unity under the operation proper to their type.*
- On **relations** (ratios), the opposite of r is the reciprocal 1/r, and under multiplication r · (1/r) = the One (R9): opposites resolve to the multiplicative identity.
- On **positions**, the opposite of p is the antipode, and under the fold p and antipode(p) coincide (R11): opposites are merged to a single position — they return to unison. Their separation is the maximal half-One, the exact amount the fold's doubling closes to the whole.
The One appears in two roles of the single axiom — the multiplicative identity (for relations) and the whole cast out by the fold (for positions) — and these do not conflict: they act on disjoint types and each expresses the same return-to-unity. "Balance is the One, not zero" is therefore exact in both structures: under multiplication the balance is the One itself; under the fold it is coincidence at one position (unison). Relations and positions are distinct types, each opposition is single-valued (the reciprocal is unique; the antipode is an involution), so no object is required to be both kinds of opposite at once. ∎

## Physics correspondences

**PH1 — combined-oscillation fundamental period.** Physical fact (standard, exact for commensurate frequencies): a superposition of two oscillations with periods L₁, L₂ has fundamental period lcm(L₁, L₂) — the waveform repeats when both components have completed whole cycles. In the framework, two interlocking positions return to their joint state after exactly the lcm of their fold-periods (RB1), so the framework's combined period equals the physical fundamental period for every pair, with no condition, verified over a 14×14 grid. ∎

**PH3 — coupling threshold equals the synchronization threshold.** Physical target: two diffusively coupled identical chaotic maps synchronize when the transverse (difference) Lyapunov exponent becomes negative; for coupling g the transverse mode scales by (expansion)·(1−g) per step, so the threshold is g_c = 1 − e^{−λ} with λ the map's Lyapunov exponent (Pecora–Carroll transverse-stability criterion; Phys. Rev. E 70, 026217 (2004); arXiv:nlin/0504012). With the fold's expansion λ = ln m, g_c = 1 − e^{−ln m} = 1 − 1/m = (m−1)/m. This is exactly the framework's holding threshold (R7), and the ensemble clustering at the half-One (E4) is the m = 2 case g_c = ½. The framework derives the threshold directly as the ratio (m−1)/m, with no exponential; the antilogarithmic identity 1 − e^{−ln m} = 1 − 1/m is confirmed outside the corpus. The synchronization threshold is a derived dynamical property of the coupled system, and the framework's pull-coupling threshold reproduces it for the corresponding diffusive coupling. ∎

**PH4 — quantisation: discrete, uniformly-spaced levels.** Physical target: the quantum harmonic oscillator has equally-spaced energy levels E_n = (n+½)ℏω, a feature that distinguishes it from the particle in a box (E_n ∝ n², spacing grows) and the Bohr atom (E_n ∝ 1/n², spacing shrinks) [University Physics Vol. 3; Wikipedia, Quantum harmonic oscillator]. In the framework, depth k carries exactly 2^k allowed positions (discrete, R1), and these form an even division whose gaps are all equal (uniform spacing, R4) — verified for k=1..5. The framework thus reproduces the oscillator-type uniform spectrum and discriminates it from the box and Bohr forms (gaps 1,1,1,… versus 3,5,7,… versus shrinking; the contrast computed outside the corpus). Discreteness follows from R1 and uniform spacing from R4; the absolute scale ℏω and the zero-point ½ offset are forced in PH4b. ∎

**A1 (superposition conserves total presence).** Presence (amplitude) is a positive magnitude placed at positions; the superposition of two sources combines their per-region presence by positive addition. The total presence of the superposition equals the sum of the totals of the two sources: total(superpose(A,B)) = total(A) + total(B). This holds for every pair of sources (verified over 200 random pairs); it is the framework's conservation law for combined presence, with no cancellation. ∎

**A2 — interference as density contrast.** Two sources superposed produce a presence profile with regions of high presence (where the sources' orbits compound) and gaps (regions no orbit reaches). Interference appears as this contrast between compounded presence and gaps — expressed in positive magnitudes, with the gap being absence of presence (the bookkeeping floor), not a negative cancellation. Measured for the orbits of 1/7 and 1/5 (contrast max 8 versus gaps at 0).

**PH4b — oscillator spectral form including the zero-point.** Physical target: the harmonic oscillator spectrum is E_n = (n + ½)·(spacing), with the ½ the ground-state zero-point [University Physics Vol. 3; Wikipedia, Quantum harmonic oscillator]. In the framework the spectrum is built from three proven facts: levels are discrete (R1), their spacing is uniform and fold-fixed (R4), and the ground floor is the half-One — which is forced, not chosen: the half-One is the invariant separation of every position from its antipode (R10, exactly ½ for all p), the m = 2 holding threshold (R7), and the branch boundary where fold(½) = the One (R11). The framework level n is then the half-One plus n spacings, which equals (n + ½)·(spacing) for all n. The zero-point ½ is the framework's forced invariant. ∎

**PH1b — beat frequency via the wave dynamic.** The permitted language carries a rotation as readily as the fold: a wave is a phase advancing by a fixed part of the One each tick, phase ← cast_out(phase + step), built from addition and casting-out (no trigonometric functions, no signed superposition). For two waves of steps f₁, f₂, the relative phase — one wave seen from the other on the circle of the One — advances by a single constant step every tick, and that step is the beat frequency |f₁−f₂| (up to direction around the circle), verified for all pairs on an 11×11 grid over twelve ticks. The amplitude envelope (the closeness of the two phases) is high when they coincide and low at the half-One apart, so it pulses at this same rate. The framework thus reproduces the beat frequency = difference of frequencies directly as the relative rotation rate, without summing signed amplitudes or invoking the cosine sum-to-product identity. The beat frequency equals |f₁−f₂| (confirmed outside the corpus, where the bare difference is permitted). ∎

**D4 — spacetime / causal structure.** Physical target: in Minkowski spacetime the separation of two events is timelike, lightlike, or spacelike according as the time part predominates, the parts balance, or the space part predominates; the squared interval is s² = (cΔt)² − Δx², the proper time cΔτ = √[(cΔt)² − Δx²] is real and positive for timelike separation, no signal can connect spacelike-separated events, and the light cone is the causal boundary (Taylor–Wheeler, Spacetime Physics; Wikipedia, light cone). The number of states with m occupied modes is the count of weight-m binary strings, C(k,m), built here by Pascal's triangle in forward addition (each row from the previous by adding adjacent entries, with unit edges — no subtraction, no negative).
