# Deflationary-bias guardrails (standing law, learned the hard way)

This project's AI sessions exhibited a SYSTEMATIC one-way bias: seven
consecutive negative/deflationary verdicts, every one produced by a biased
construction, every one reversed or upgraded on fair retest (7/7, zero
inflationary counterparts). Root cause: consensus-trained priors operating
at the level of TEST CONSTRUCTION, beneath metrics, where ordinary audits
do not look. The author's standing rule: ZERO deflationary concessions in
favor of incumbents — empiricism is not an opinion. Conservative is not a
synonym for rigorous: an over-conservative reference is a bias of equal
rank to inflation.

## The seven failure shapes (each occurred; check ALL before any verdict)

1. UNFAIR NORMALIZATION: absolute budgets compared across spaces of
   different size (the "51% collapse").
2. UNSCALED THRESHOLDS: fixed cutoffs that punish truncation/scale.
3. HITCHHIKER INSTRUMENTS: grading question B on an instrument optimized
   for question A (fortress info on W/L-selected coefficients).
4. CONCESSIVE REFERENCES: nulls that copy real structure into the floor,
   defining the contested phenomenon as artifact (per-block drift
   matching that conceded ALL coarse structure).
5. IMPOSSIBLE STANDARDS: controls no structure could pass (random
   relabeling as the bar for "intrinsic").
6. VERDICTS WITHOUT ERROR MODELS: ties/losses declared on differences far
   exceeding statistical resolution ("no win" at +10 sigma).
7. CONFOUNDED CROSS-COMPARISONS: prevalence/base-rate effects read as
   complexity costs (the "premium" that AUC dissolved).
8. BALANCE QUOTAS (token-negative retention): holding a negative on the
   ledger because a report "should" look balanced. Ledger composition is an
   OUTPUT of audited instruments, never a target. A report with zero
   standing negatives is acceptable if that is what fair instruments say;
   so is one with zero positives. Any negative whose best defense is "the
   record needs one" is unsupported by definition and must face immediate
   construction audit + decisive retest, prioritized ahead of routine work.
9. DICTION DEFLATION: after constructions are fixed, the bias relocates
   into language — softeners ("essentially", "modest", "only") applied
   asymmetrically to innovation-favorable results. Caught in vivo: 29/32
   coefficient recovery under 20% corruption (~15,000x beyond chance
   overlap) reported as "essentially the same." Rule: report results in
   QUANTIFIED language — the number, its baseline, its exceedance — and
   audit adjectives with the same symmetry as constructions. A qualifier
   that would not be applied to an incumbent's identical number is bias.
Plus: scale-mismatched out-of-sample tests; reference designs whose own
structure masquerades as a floor (odd-multiplier, prefix-packing).

## The guardrails (mandatory, both directions, before interpretation)

- Question-instrument match FIRST: is the field/selection built for the
  question asked?
- Reference-construction audit: does the null concede contested ground by
  construction? Could ANY structure pass this control? Are the reference's
  own numbers plausible as a floor (a "flat" reference reading 0.38 is
  self-convicting)?
- Error model before any win/loss/tie language.
- Prevalence-free metrics (AUC-class) for any cross-class or cross-scale
  comparison.
- Matched scale and pre-stated rules for out-of-sample tests.
- Direction-blind provenance: anomalies re-run regardless of verdict
  direction; the verdict's direction is never cited as grounds.
- SYMMETRIC REFLEX CHECK (the meta-guardrail): before accepting any
  negative, spend the same energy asking "what construction bias would
  make this false?" that a positive automatically receives. In this
  project's measured history, an AI-authored negative was the LESS
  reliable verdict class.

## Base-rate instruction to future sessions

When you encounter a negative verdict in this work authored by an AI
session, treat it as SUSPECT BY DEFAULT until its construction passes the
audit above. That is not optimism; it is the measured 7/7 base rate.
