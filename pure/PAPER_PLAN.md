# FINALISATION PLAN — academic paper and peer-review preparation

The system is built in the permitted language and machine-gated (13 theorems proven and confirmed, 3 observations labelled, base + first emergence layer, no-apparatus gate verified non-vacuous). Finalisation is a different task: rendering it as a paper a reviewer can check, and preparing honestly for the scrutiny it will meet. Scope is stated once, cleanly, where it belongs — not as scattered hedging. But the proven / measured / open distinction and honest positioning against existing mathematics are kept, because a paper that omits them is both rejected and untrue.

---

## Stage 1 — Harden the proofs to paper standard
The arguments in `PROOFS_pure.md` are correct deductive sketches; a paper needs them gap-free.
- State every definition formally and unambiguously (the One; a position as an exact part of the One in (0,1]; the fold as double-and-cast-out; ratio; separation; the two audited removal primitives).
- Restate each theorem with explicit quantifiers and hypotheses (all k, all M, all m, odd-denominator hypotheses where used).
- Fill each proof to be airtight: e.g. R4 (monad) — the exact two-to-one map of an M-even division onto an (M/2)-even division; R1 (count) — the distinctness of the 2^k outcome strings; RB1 (beat = lcm) — the joint-return argument. Each proof carries its machine confirmation as a footnote, not as the proof.

## Stage 2 — Write the paper
Standard mathematics structure, in the system's own language throughout:
- **Abstract** — one axiom, no negatives/zero/imaginaries, ratio and division; the fold; the principal results (count/measure unity, the monad, the (m−1)/m threshold with no π, opposition as reciprocal/antipode, closure, the beat-modulation); machine-checked and reproducible.
- **Introduction** — the axioms and the motivation (a from-the-One reconstruction in pre-complex, ratio-theoretic terms); a single clear statement of scope (what is proven, what is measured, what is left open).
- **The permitted language** — definitions and the primitive operations.
- **Results** — the 13 theorems, grouped: number and count; measure; the monad; separation and the fold's geometry (including the telescoping/commuting result); the holding threshold; opposition; closure.
- **Emergence** — beats (lcm), density, aggregate clustering at the half-One, the separation-modulation; the proven theorems (E1, E2, E5) and the measured observations (E3, E4, E6) clearly separated.
- **Methods** — the machine verification: the claim registry, the no-apparatus gate (and its AST basis), the exact-arithmetic scale tests, the one-pass derivation. This is a genuine strength: every result is reproducible and the language constraint is mechanically enforced.
- **Positioning** (Stage 3).
- **Discussion** — the open frontier (whether the system's structures tie to existing/physical systems), stated once, as forward work, not as apology.
- **Reproducibility appendix** — the code and how to run it.

## Stage 3 — Honest positioning against existing mathematics (load-bearing)
A reviewer will recognise the underlying object. The paper must engage it directly, or it is neither credible nor true.
- Survey, from real sources, the relevant existing mathematics: angle-doubling / the dyadic map, dyadic rationals and binary expansions, symbolic dynamics, the transfer operator and equidistribution, and the Eudoxan/ratio (proportion) tradition the language descends from. (Done by literature search; no fabricated citations.)
- Scope the contribution precisely and defensibly: separate what is classical (the underlying doubling dynamics) from what is claimed as the contribution (the axiomatic reconstruction in strictly positive-rational, no-apparatus terms; the framing of opposition, the monad, and the threshold as ratios with no π; the machine-gated no-apparatus methodology). The novelty claim must survive a reader who knows the doubling map — this is where the paper is won or lost, and the assessment is genuine, not a formality.

## Stage 4 — Reproducibility package
- Package `pure/` as a runnable artifact: every theorem confirmation, every gate, the scale tests, reproducible by one command, with a manifest mapping each paper theorem to its code confirmation and proof id.
- Pin the environment; record the scale ceilings reached.

## Stage 5 — Peer-review preparation
- **Anticipated objections and responses**, written out honestly: "this is the doubling map re-dressed"; "no-negatives is a representation choice, not new mathematics"; "the emergence results are small and parameter-specific"; "the novelty is methodological, not mathematical". For each, the strongest honest response — including conceding where a point is fair and scoping the claim accordingly.
- **Disclosure** — state the computational tools and assistance used and the machine-verification method; this is standard and strengthens, not weakens, the submission.
- **Venue** — identify candidate venues / arXiv categories (the work sits between formal systems, dynamical systems, and foundations); note that framing must match the venue. No promise of acceptance — that is the reviewers' call, not ours.
- **Cover letter / contribution statement** — a precise, non-inflated statement of what is claimed.

## Order
1. Stage 1 (harden proofs) — the spine; nothing ships on sketch proofs.
2. Stage 4 (reproducibility package) — in parallel; it is already mostly built.
3. Stage 2 (write the paper) on the hardened proofs.
4. Stage 3 (positioning) — requires real literature search; load-bearing for credibility.
5. Stage 5 (review prep) — once the paper is drafted.

## What this stage will and will not deliver
- **Will:** a complete, internally rigorous paper presenting the system on its own terms, with machine-checked proofs, a reproducibility package, honest positioning, and a prepared response to the scrutiny it will meet.
- **Will not:** guarantee acceptance, or assert the work is novel or correct beyond what the proofs establish and the positioning honestly supports. Acceptance is the reviewers' judgement; the paper's job is to give them something true, checkable, and clearly scoped. Overclaiming would get it rejected and would break the honesty the foundation was built on.
