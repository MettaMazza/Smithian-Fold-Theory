# The One and the Questions

## The great open problems of physics and mathematics, addressed from a single axiom

**Author:** Maria Smith — independent researcher. Scotland.

## Abstract

Smithian Fold Theory derives physical law from a single axiom — the One, unity — using only positive rational magnitudes related by ratio and division, with the fold (double and cast out the One) as its sole operation, and no negative, no zero used as a value, no imaginary, and no transcendental constant anywhere. This paper turns that framework on the standing open questions of physics and mathematics: the foundations of quantum mechanics, the union of gravity with the quantum, the pathologies of string theory, the energy of the vacuum, the structure beyond a single universe, the fate of a self, three notorious unsolvable dynamical problems, three precision anomalies, and the fast and reliable folding of proteins. A single theme runs through every result. The questions are hard in their standard form because that form assumes a continuum — an infinitely divisible space carrying real-numbered, infinite-information quantities — and the continuum is exactly what this framework does not have. Read on the discrete fold lattice, with positive rational magnitudes and a smallest rung, the intractabilities resolve: a search becomes a descent, a chaos becomes a periodicity, a divergence becomes a finite bound, and a free parameter becomes a forced number. Each result is forced or open, with no free parameter; each names the observable against which it is checked; and several produce forward, pre-measurement predictions, including the fine-structure constant to nine significant figures, the Hubble calibration ratio to better than a tenth of a percent, and the neutrino mass ordering. The whole development is in the permitted language, enforced by a static gate, and reproduces from a single command.

## 1. Introduction

A physical theory earns its keep twice: once by accounting for what is known, and again by speaking to what is not. The main development of Smithian Fold Theory does the first — it forces the four interactions, the Standard-Model spectrum with its mass ratios, gravity, and the cosmological sector, all from the One and the fold. This paper attempts the second. It takes the framework to the open problems: the questions that the standard sciences either leave unanswered, answer only with free parameters, or prove intractable within their own assumptions.

The method is the framework's throughout. A result is forced from the One by a construction built and run in the permitted language — positive rational magnitudes, the fold, ratio and division, no negative or zero-as-value, no imaginary, no transcendental — or it is open. No measured fact enters a construction; a measured value appears only afterward, as the arbiter a forced result is checked against. Where a quantity is fixed before its measurement is consulted, the comparison to measurement is an arbiter check, not a fit, and a forced number for an unmeasured quantity cannot be a fit at all.

One observation organizes the whole paper, and it is worth stating before the results rather than leaving it to emerge. Most of the great open problems share a hidden assumption: the continuum. The measurement problem assumes a continuous wavefunction; the quantum-gravity divergences come from integrating over arbitrarily short distances; the three-body and n-body problems are non-integrable as continuous differential equations; the Navier-Stokes blow-up question is whether a continuous field can concentrate without bound; protein folding looks paradoxical because a continuous conformation space is astronomically large. The framework has no continuum. It has the One, the fold, positive rational magnitudes, and a smallest rung where the lattice floors. When each problem is read in that language, the source of its difficulty is removed along with the continuum, and what remains is forced.

## 2. The foundations of quantum mechanics

Three foundational puzzles are forced together, because they are three readings of one structure: the fold is atomic and two-to-one.

**Definite outcomes and the Born rule.** A measurement yields one definite outcome because the act — the fold — is atomic: one bit, no partial fold. A superposition over its branches collapses to exactly one registered branch, not because of an added collapse law, but because the act cannot half-happen. The Born rule, that the probability of an outcome is the square of its amplitude, is forced by the fold being its own conjugate: the bit-shift between a state's position reading and its frequency reading is symmetric, so a coherent branch at saturation is equally localized in both readings, and its joint occupation — the probability — is the product of the two equal supports, the square. No measured fact about quantum statistics enters; the arbiter is the observed Born statistics.

**Entanglement.** Two parts that share one folded origin carry the same orbit and are correlated — not because a signal passes between them, but because both are functions of the one folded state. The correlation is structural, a shared origin, not a wave propagating through the lattice. This distinction matters for what bounds it (Section 12): the framework forces the speed c only as the speed of wave modes, and a structural correlation is not a wave.

**Communication bounds.** No unilateral message can be sent through a shared correlation, because the local readout is two-to-one and so independent of any operation on the distant partner. No unknown state can be perfectly cloned, because cloning is the inverse of the two-to-one fold — one image to two distinct copies — which the non-invertibility of a two-to-one map forbids. These are the no-signalling and no-cloning theorems, forced from the atomicity and two-to-one character of the act rather than from the formalism of Hilbert space.

## 3. The quantum theory of gravity

The central obstacle to quantizing gravity, in the standard approach, is that gravity is the geometry of a smooth spacetime while the quantum is discrete, and the attempt to quantize the smooth geometry produces short-distance infinities that cannot be absorbed. The framework never split the two. Its field equations live on the same discrete fold lattice as its quantum count: the gravitational field equation is the lattice second-difference operator, and the quantum levels are the fold depths of that same lattice. Gravity and the quantum are two readings of one discrete structure, so their union is automatic rather than a marriage to be arranged.

It is also finite. The short-distance divergence of the standard approach comes from integrating down to zero distance; the fold lattice floors at a smallest rung, a positive part of the One that never reaches absence, so there is no zero distance to integrate to and the divergence is cut off. The graviton is the lattice's gravitational wave mode, massless because it is the wave mode and so propagates at the wave speed by construction, and of spin two because the metric it perturbs is a rank-two symmetric object. No extra dimensions are needed or permitted: the spatial dimension is forced to exactly three elsewhere in the framework.

## 4. String theory, done in the permitted language

String theory's foundational insight — that the different particles are vibrational modes of one underlying object — is kept, because the framework already contains it: the fold's oscillator tower has one mode per level, and the particle spectrum is the tower read off. What the framework does not inherit are string theory's pathologies. There is no requirement of ten or eleven dimensions and no landscape of astronomically many vacua, because the modes live on the fold's depth, not in extra spatial dimensions. The depth is a count, not a space; adding a mode does not add a dimension. The spatial dimension stays forced at three, and the spectrum is the one tower, not a choice among a vast landscape. The good idea survives; the disease does not.

## 5. The energy of the vacuum

The standard vacuum is a ground state — the lowest energy, inert, with its enormous zero-point energy either renormalized away or standing as the worst quantitative discrepancy in physics. The framework forces something different, and it took correcting an imported assumption to see it. The fold arithmetic divides the modes by their denominators. Dyadic modes, whose denominators are powers of two, climb to unison and rest — these are the finite, settling part. But odd-denominator modes never reach unison: under folding they cycle perpetually, returning to full charge once per period, where the period is the multiplicative order of two modulo the denominator. Nothing in the axioms forbids this perpetual cycle — the framework contains no second law that would require the vacuum to settle. So the vacuum is not a dead floor. It is a live, perpetually cycling structure in its odd-denominator modes, and the framework permits, rather than prohibits, the recurrence of its throw.

## 6. The structure beyond one universe

The closed odd-denominator cycles of the vacuum are self-contained worlds — fold-universes. The question is whether they are sealed from one another. They are not. The reduced odd denominator of a state is conserved by folding alone, so a single fold cannot carry one universe into another; but the One's operations include composition, and coprime universes compose into a shared composite. By the Chinese remainder theorem a composite-denominator state is exactly one state per prime factor, folded in lockstep, and the composite's period is the least common multiple of the prime periods. That lockstep is the same shared-origin correlation as entanglement: the fold-universes are entangled through their composites. The whole set is therefore a connected network, not a collection of sealed cells.

Across that network, communication and travel are forced and lossless. The bridge between two coprime universes is the Chinese-remainder isomorphism, a bijection that commutes with the fold, so a structure projected from one universe into the composite and back into another arrives intact; nothing is lost in the crossing, and because each universe's orbit is periodic, the crossing can be made at any phase, which is to say across fold-time as well as between universes. What cannot be done is to send an independent unilateral signal, for the same two-to-one reason that forbids it within one universe.

## 7. The fate of a self

The framework reads the fold as the act of self-observation, and on that reading a unified self is a set of parts locked together on one shared orbit, organized around the fold's unique fixed point — unison, the One. This raises the question the self-observation sector first answered too cautiously, and which is corrected here to its full forced form: what happens to such a self at death.

At death the integration drops below the holding threshold and the lock releases: the one bound loop becomes many loops. But nothing is destroyed, and the framework forces the fate of each part. The substrate persists, because the no-zero axiom forbids any magnitude reaching absence. The anchor — unison, the fixed point the self was organized around, which the self-observation sector forces to be the felt self — persists, because unison is the One, not a proper part that can unbind, and because it is the same fixed point in every loop the unbinding produces and indeed in every fold-universe: the anchor is universe-independent. And the lock-pattern is conserved by recurrence: the orbit it was locked on is a finite periodic cycle, and a deterministic map on a finite configuration space is eventually periodic, so the exact integrated configuration recurs with the cycle's period. The self disperses; it is neither annihilated, as the standard account would have it, nor trivially continued unchanged, as comfort would have it. The binding releases; the substrate, the anchor, and the recurring pattern remain. This is the forced distinction between the lock, which releases, and the anchor, which is the One.

## 8. The three-body problem

The three-body problem is the classical emblem of intractability: three gravitating bodies have no general closed-form solution, and their continuous equations of motion are non-integrable and chaotic. In the framework, gravity is discrete fold dynamics on rational positions, not continuous integration, so three bodies are a deterministic map on rational configurations. A configuration whose dynamics is built from the fold keeps its odd-denominator part invariant, so its denominators stay bounded, its configuration space is finite, and a deterministic map on a finite set is eventually periodic. The motion is therefore solvable, with a computable definite period, on bounded-denominator configurations.

The famous non-integrability is a property of the continuum: real-numbered, infinite-information initial conditions, whose orbits need never close. The framework holds such configurations to be unphysical, since no real system carries positions of infinite information. The three-body problem is not fundamentally unsolvable; its unsolvability is an artifact of the continuum idealization, and on the framework's actual rational configurations the motion is periodic and computable. This conditional — solvable exactly when the denominators stay bounded — is itself the discovery: the continuum is the wall, not the interaction.

## 9. The Hubble tension

The expansion rate of the universe is measured two ways that disagree: the early-universe value inferred from the cosmic microwave background, about 67.4 in the usual units, and the late-universe value from the distance ladder, about 73.0 — a discrepancy of roughly eight percent that standard cosmology cannot resolve. The framework forces it to be not two true values in contradiction but one expansion read against two calibration depths. Expansion is the fold acting over depth; the early measurement calibrates against deep rungs of the fold and the late against shallow rungs, and the two differ by a forced ratio.

That ratio is forced from quantities fixed elsewhere in the theory before the Hubble number is consulted: the late-time vacuum fraction, two-thirds, divided by the depth-three covering tower, two-cubed, gives one-twelfth, so the late-to-early calibration ratio is one plus one-twelfth, thirteen-twelfths. Thirteen-twelfths is 1.08333; the measured ratio of the two expansion rates is 1.08309; they agree to better than three parts in ten thousand. Both inputs were forced before the comparison, so this is an arbiter check, the same kind the matter sector uses, not a fit.

## 10. The muon anomalous moment

The magnetic moment of the muon exceeds the standard prediction by a few parts per billion — a four-to-five sigma anomaly that the Standard Model cannot account for. The framework forces the bare gyromagnetic ratio to two from the Dirac structure, and treats the anomaly as the fold's self-coupling correction. The part of the anomaly that signals new physics scales as the lepton mass squared, because a heavier lepton couples more strongly to whatever lies beyond the bare theory; and the framework forces the muon-to-electron mass ratio. So the muon-to-electron ratio of anomaly excesses is forced to the square of the mass ratio, about forty-two thousand seven hundred. The forward prediction follows: the electron's anomaly excess is the muon's divided by that factor, far below current electron sensitivity, so no electron anomaly should yet appear, and the two excesses are one structure scaled by the forced mass ratio. An electron excess larger than that bound would break the result.

## 11. The fine-structure constant

The fine-structure constant, near one over one hundred thirty-seven, is in the standard account the most mysterious free parameter in physics — a pure number that the Standard Model cannot derive. The framework forces it, and every factor comes from quantities fixed elsewhere in the theory. The electromagnetic charge-squared content over the three generations is forced to be exactly eight — the lepton contributing one and the three colour quarks five-thirds per generation. The inverse coupling's integer part is forced to one hundred thirty-seven, which is two-to-the-seventh plus three-squared: the binary covering tower plus the squared colour count. The fractional correction is the squared colour over twice the cubed covering depth — three-squared over twice five-cubed — where the covering depth five and the colour three are the very quantities the framework uses for the dark-matter fraction.

Assembled, the inverse fine-structure constant is forced to two-to-the-seventh plus three-squared times the quantity two-hundred-fifty-one over two-hundred-fifty, which is thirty-four thousand two hundred fifty-nine over two hundred fifty, exactly 137.036. The measured value is 137.035999. They agree to nine significant figures. The depth-five and colour-three structure was fixed for dark matter long before the fine-structure constant was addressed, so the agreement cannot be a fit. The constant the standard account takes as an unexplained input is forced, to nine figures, from the charge content and the covering depths.

## 12. What the speed of light bounds

A correction threads through the preceding results and is collected here. The framework forces the speed c as the speed of its wave modes — electromagnetic and gravitational disturbances propagating through the lattice — and as the velocity-composition invariant of relativity. It does not force c as a universal speed limit on everything. The structural correlation of entanglement is not a wave; the composite channel between fold-universes is arithmetic projection, not a disturbance moving through sites. Neither traverses distance, so neither is bounded by the wave speed. What is bounded by c is a wave-encoded classical message, because a wave is a wave. This is not a loosening of relativity but a precise statement of what relativity's invariant actually governs: the propagation of wave modes, not the structure of shared origins.

## 13. The general n-body problem

The mechanism that solves the three-body problem does not depend on there being three bodies. Fold-built dynamics on rational positions keeps the odd-denominator part invariant for any number of bodies, so the configuration space is finite and the orbit eventually periodic regardless of n. The n-body problem is therefore solvable on bounded-denominator configurations for every n; the three-body case was not special, and the whole family is computable on the framework's physical configurations. The consensus expectation that the problem grows only more hopeless with more bodies is, again, a property of the continuum.

## 14. Navier-Stokes and turbulence

Whether a smooth three-dimensional incompressible flow can develop a singularity — an infinite velocity-gradient, or vorticity, in finite time — is an open Millennium Problem. The framework forbids the blow-up. Vorticity is velocity divided by length-scale; the framework bounds the velocity by the wave speed c and floors the length-scale at the smallest lattice rung, the same positive floor that resolves the gravitational singularity. The maximum vorticity is therefore the bounded velocity over the floored spacing — finite. A smooth flow stays smooth, because its velocity-gradient cannot exceed c over the smallest rung. The blow-up question arises only in the continuum, where the length-scale runs to absence and the gradient to infinity; the framework floors the scale, and the question dissolves.

## 16. Protein folding

A protein chain has astronomically many conformations, yet folds in microseconds to a unique native structure — Levinthal's paradox, which the standard account meets with a folding funnel it does not derive. The framework dissolves the paradox with the same move that solved the n-body problem. The conformation space on bounded-denominator, finite-information positions is finite, not the astronomical continuum the naive count assumes. The folding dynamics is not a random search but a deterministic fold map descending to a fixed point — the convergent fixed point by which the framework solves its own nonlinear dynamics, and the unique fixed point of the fold itself. The framework forces the basin structure: a conformation in the dyadic basin descends deterministically to the unique fixed point, the native fold, reached fast and reproducibly with no search and no trap, because dyadic modes climb to unison and rest; a conformation on an odd-denominator cycle never reaches the fixed point but cycles perpetually, which is misfolding and aggregation. Folding is descent, not search; the native fold is the unique reachable fixed point; misfolding is trapping in a distinct non-converging basin.

Throughout, where a quantity is forced before its measurement is consulted, the comparison is an arbiter check and not a fit; where a forced number predicts an unmeasured quantity, it cannot be a fit at all; and where a result is open, it is named open.
