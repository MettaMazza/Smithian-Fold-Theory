# Phase Two Plan — Establishing the Remaining Physical Domains

*Author: Maria Smith. Claude provides operational assistance only — it builds, proves, gates, and reports; it does not decide what is established or what the work is worth (GUIDANCE §1, §9, §10).*

## Governance (reinforced — GUIDANCE §10)

1. A domain is **established** only on the Phase-One standard: built in the permitted language (one axiom; no negatives, no zero, no imaginaries; ratio and division), proven, scanned clean by the no-apparatus gate with the integrity tripwire intact, and confirmed against cited physical facts.
2. Each domain — and each part within it — is established **before** the next is begun. The sequence below is a dependency order.
3. No domain is cleared by fabrication, fitting, or relabelling. If a domain cannot be established at the current extent, the wall and its specific cause are reported, and the build stops there. A wall reported honestly is the correct outcome.
4. The instrument builds and reports; the author and the assessors decide what is established, whether a wall is acceptable, and which domain to enter next.

## Phase Two — domain sequence

Each domain lists: what it builds on, what is constructed in the permitted language, the criterion for calling it established, and the honest contingency (each is a target; some carry a high risk of a wall, which will be reported rather than forced).

### D1 — Fields: the coupled lattice
- **Builds on:** wave (PH1b), coupling/sync (PH3), amplitude.
- **Construct:** a lattice of waves on the One, diffusively coupled in the permitted language; many sites, each a rotating phase, coupled to neighbours by the pull primitive.
- **Establish:** propagation of a disturbance along the lattice; the dispersion relation (mode rate vs mode index) in ratio form; the normal modes as the discrete spectrum (tie to PH4). Confirm against the standard coupled-oscillator-chain (monatomic lattice) dispersion [cite at build].
- **Criterion:** proven from the axioms + gate-clean + confirmed against the cited lattice dispersion.
- **Contingency:** the conventional dispersion uses a sine and signed differences; the mode structure must be derived in ratio/proportion. If a clean ratio dispersion does not emerge, the wall is reported.

### D2 — Continuum field / propagation law
- **Builds on:** D1.
- **Construct:** the many-site limit of the coupled lattice; the signal-propagation law as a ratio (a finite propagation speed).
- **Establish:** a forced finite propagation speed; the field's continuum behaviour expressed in the permitted language.
- **Contingency:** the conventional wave equation is a signed second-order differential equation; deriving propagation without signed second differences is the challenge. Possible wall.

### D3 — Interaction / mode coupling
- **Builds on:** D1, D2.
- **Construct:** coupling between field modes (mode–mode interaction) in the permitted language.
- **Establish:** an interaction structure; any **forced** dimensionless coupling ratio — the first place a coupling constant could arise (toward PH5).
- **Contingency:** a coupling constant counts only if forced by the construction; fitting a value is barred (Texas-sharpshooter).

### D4 — Spacetime / causal structure
- **Builds on:** D2 (a finite propagation speed defines a causal cone).
- **Construct:** an interval over space-and-step from ratios; causal ordering from the finite propagation speed.
- **Establish:** an invariant tied to the propagation speed; a causal structure.
- **Contingency:** the Lorentzian interval carries a signature (a sign difference between space and time); building causal structure without a signed metric is a major challenge — the likely first hard wall, reported honestly if reached.

### D5 — Relativity
- **Builds on:** D4 + the invariant speed.
- **Establish:** invariance of the propagation speed across frames; transformation of intervals; time dilation as a ratio.
- **Contingency:** wholly dependent on D4; speculative until D4 is established.

### D6 — Quantum structure beyond the oscillator
- **Builds on:** amplitude/occupancy, discreteness (R1), spectrum (PH4), the fold's two-preimage fibre (R11).
- **Establish:** a complementarity/uncertainty bound as a ratio inequality; measurement as occupancy resolving to one branch of the fold fibre.
- **Contingency:** the uncertainty bound must be derived as a ratio, not assumed; target.

### D7 — Quantum numbers / particle content
- **Builds on:** discreteness (2^k), branch counts, the half-One (R10).
- **Establish:** discrete quantum numbers from the branch structure; test whether a spin-½-like quantity is **forced** by the half-One.
- **Contingency:** matching the half-One to spin-½ is barred unless forced by a derivation; likely waits on D5 (relativistic spin); wall reported if not forced.

### D8 — Fundamental dimensionless constants (closes PH5)
- **Builds on:** D3, D4 (the interaction/field domain where a coupling constant lives).
- **Establish:** a forced dimensionless constant from the interaction structure.
- **Contingency:** attempted only after the domain exists; fitting to a known value is barred.

### D9 — Gravity
- **Builds on:** D4 spacetime + a mass-energy notion.
- **Establish:** a curvature/attraction structure from ratios.
- **Contingency:** the farthest target; may be unreachable in this framework; reported as a wall if so.

### D10 — The strong interaction
- **Builds on:** D7 (the fold's m-to-1 fibre), D3 (mode coupling), D9d (the flux law).
- **Already established (structural):** D7b — the m-fold fibre forces exactly m internal charge kinds, three for the tripling fold (colour), neutral at whole triples. D7d — flux confined to a tube (effective dimension one) gives a constant force and an unbounded, linear separation energy: confinement, against the d=3 Coulomb field that converges.
- **Construct (remaining):** the self-coupling of the mediating field — the carrier itself carrying colour, so the field sources itself (the nonlinear gravity self-sourcing of D9l–D9n is the in-language pattern to reuse); the running of the strong coupling toward the forced threshold (m−1)/m=2/3 at m=3 (PH3/PH5 give the threshold; the running is the reach); the dynamical formation of the flux tube from the self-coupled field rather than imposed as geometry.
- **Establish:** a self-sourcing mediator in the permitted language; a coupling that strengthens with separation (the engine showing the carry factor approaching the One as the copies separate); the flux tube emerging from the self-coupling, not assumed.
- **Criterion:** each part proven from the axioms, gate-clean with the tripwire intact, confirmed against the cited strong-interaction relationship, registered in `claims_physics.py`.
- **Contingency:** the colour gauge group's full non-abelian structure conventionally uses signed structure constants and matrix commutators; deriving the self-coupling in ratio/proportion is the challenge. The reachable reductions (self-sourcing by reuse of D9l–D9n; the threshold already forced) are attempted first; a wall is reported only at the specific in-language obstruction shown in code (§12).

### D11 — The weak interaction and electroweak structure
- **Builds on:** D7c (chirality from the fold's two preimages), D5 (relativity), EM1–EM6 (the electromagnetic sector it unifies with), D9d (the flux/range law).
- **Already established (structural):** D7c — the fold's 2-to-1 fibre carries a two-valued handedness; a single-handed coupling acts on one preimage of the antipodal pair only (parity asymmetry).
- **Construct (remaining):** a short-ranged force from a massive mediator — the range set by a mass scale in the permitted language (the field that does not reach the long-range 1/r^(d−1) form of the massless mediator, EM/gravity), derived without an exponential (the in-language route is a threshold/cutoff from the magnitude engine, not e^{−mr}); the mixing of the weak and electromagnetic sectors as one structure with a forced mixing ratio (a single coupling splitting into two, the way PH5 forces (m−1)/m); the symmetry-breaking that distinguishes the massive mediators from the massless one.
- **Establish:** a forced short range tied to a mass scale; a forced electroweak mixing ratio (no measured angle fed in); the massless/massive split as a structural result, not an input.
- **Criterion:** each part proven, gate-clean, confirmed against the cited weak/electroweak relationship, registered.
- **Contingency:** the massive-mediator range is conventionally an exponential Yukawa screening — forbidden apparatus; the reachable reduction is a threshold or finite-range cutoff in ratio form. If no clean in-language range emerges, the wall is reported at that specific point (§12). The mixing ratio counts only if forced, never fitted (§11).

## Force parity — the criterion before Phase Three
The four fundamental interactions are to be represented at equal depth before Phase Three is entered. "Equal depth" is the Phase-One standard applied per force: each force's structure **and** its interaction dynamics built in the permitted language, proven, gate-clean, confirmed, and registered — not one force carried to full dynamics while another stands as structure only.

- **Electromagnetism** — established across structure and dynamics (EM1–EM6: Coulomb, magnetism-as-relativity, the wave at c, 2D and 3D Maxwell, the Lorentz force).
- **Gravity** — established across structure and dynamics (D9–D9q: equivalence principle, Poisson and inverse-square, waves at c, forced spatial dimension, quadrupole radiation, curvature=source in 2D/3D, nonlinear self-sourcing, the tensor with Bianchi conservation, the Schwarzschild metric, the continuum limit).
- **Strong** — structure established (D7b colour count and neutrality; D7d confinement potential); **dynamics remaining** (D10: self-coupling, running, dynamical flux tube).
- **Weak** — structure established (D7c chirality and single-handed coupling); **dynamics remaining** (D11: massive-mediator short range, electroweak mixing, the massless/massive split).

Phase Three is not entered until D10 and D11 stand on the same footing as EM and gravity. The gravitational sector's depth (D9–D9q) is the bar each other force is brought to; where a force cannot reach that bar at the current extent, the specific in-language obstruction is reported in code (§12), and the author decides — Claude does not rule a force complete or a wall acceptable (§10.4).
