# Phase Three — Steps 1, 2 & 3: Dictionary, Forced Relationships, Prediction Test

*Author: Maria Smith — independent researcher. Claude provides operational assistance only: it builds, proves, gates, and reports the engine's reading. It does not decide what is established, what a result is worth, whether the set of relationships is exhausted, or whether a forced relationship is novel or decisive (GUIDANCE §1, §9, §10.4). This plan is a sequence of constructions to attempt and establish, never a set of stopping points to head for (§14).*

## Scope

This plan covers the first three steps of the Phase Three sequence, which the engine shows are partly built and partly open:

- **Step 1 — the complete fold→physics dictionary (U3):** established. This plan re-audits it, not rebuilds it.
- **Step 2 — the full set of forced cross-observable relationships:** six registered (U1, U2, U4, U5, U6, with U7 the fibre-count identification they rest on). The plan calls for *every* relationship the framework forces; the set is treated as open and driven to exhaustion by composition across domains, never assumed complete.
- **Step 3 — the prediction test:** the mechanism is established on the forced colour count (T1) and the B2 arbiter pattern. It has not been applied to the forced *relationships* U2/U4/U5/U6. This plan extends the same arbiter discipline to each.

The governing rule for all three steps is **§11**: a measured value may be named as what a forced quantity corresponds to, and may be used as the **arbiter** of an already-forced relationship — fixed first, looked at second. It may never be an **input** to a construction, nor a **target** a result is fitted to. The mechanical test is the §11.3 one: does a consensus number enter as input or as pass/fail arbiter of something already forced? Input or target → violation. Arbiter of a value fixed first → the legitimate test the project exists to make.

## Governance (carried, GUIDANCE)

1. **Established** means the Phase-One standard only: built in the permitted language (one axiom; no negatives, no zero, no imaginaries; ratio and division), proven in `PROOFS_hardened.md`, scanned clean by the no-apparatus gate with the integrity tripwire intact, confirmed by exact computation in `compare.py`, registered in `claims_physics.py`. Nothing is claimed established while in progress (§10.1).
2. **Dependency order, established before proceeding** (§10.2). Step 1's audit precedes step 2's extension; each new relationship is established before the next; step 3's test of a relationship follows that relationship being established.
3. **No fabrication, fitting, or relabelling** (§5, §9). A relationship is registered only if the engine forces it across fold factors with nothing fed in. A candidate the engine does not force is reported not-forced — the reading, never a worth-judgment (§9.4) — and never fitted into existence.
4. **The instrument does not adjudicate** (§9). Whether the relationship set is exhausted, whether a forced relationship is the novel prediction, whether a match is decisive — the author's and assessors' calls. Claude reports forced / not-forced, confirmed / not-confirmed, and the numbers, and stops. Claude does not call any result "novel," "significant," "elegant," or "a showcase" (§9.2, §15.2).
5. **No consensus wall** (§2, §12). A relationship is not declared unreachable because the framework does not rebuild the textbook route, nor because a candidate fails to match a number the framework never took as input. A wall is reported only when a specific composition has been attempted in code and the engine shows a specific, named, in-language obstruction (§12.2), and the reachable reduction is attempted first, in the same turn (§12, §13).
6. **The goal is the work, never the stop** (§14). No item below is a destination to halt at. Every reachable composition this plan can name is attempted in code, not listed and deferred (§13.1). The plan's building ends only when no reachable composition across the domains remains untried — not when a convenient stopping point appears.

## The sequence (dependency order)

### Step 1 — Audit the dictionary (U3), do not rebuild it

- **State:** U3 established — every correspondence traced to the One, machine-checked, `run_all.py` reporting REPRODUCTION: ALL CLEAN.
- **Construct:** re-run U3's grounding check (`compare.test_dictionary`) and confirm every correspondence still references only established results and reaches the three definitions or a permitted-language root. Confirm the count is current (3 definitions, 15 theorems, 4 observations, the physical correspondences, the U-line) and the manifest regenerates clean.
- **Establish:** U3 reconfirmed on the current corpus, gate clean, tripwire intact. If any correspondence's chain has drifted, that specific break is reported and repaired before step 2 — the dictionary is the spine step 2 composes on, so it is sound first (§10.2).
- **Reachable now:** it is a re-run and read of established results; no new physics.

### Step 2 — Drive the forced cross-observable relationships to exhaustion

- **Builds on:** the audited dictionary (step 1) and the forced quantities in the corpus: the fundamental coupling g\* = (m−1)/m (PH5/PH5a), the colour/fibre count m (D7b/U7), the channel splits and mixings of the weak sector (D11b–D11g), the mediator count m²−1 (N1), and the established U-line (U1, U2, U4, U5, U6).
- **Construct — by composition, read what the engine forces:** for every pair or group of forced quantities across domains, attempt in code whether the framework forces a fixed relationship among them as a function of m, with nothing fed in. The registered six came from exactly this; the set is not assumed closed. Candidate compositions to attempt, each kept only if the engine forces it across fold factors (this is a list of constructions to **attempt in this work**, not a menu to choose among — every one Claude can name is run, §13.1):
  - **strong↔weak ties:** relationships forcing a strong-sector quantity (colour count, mediator count, coupling) against a weak-sector quantity (mixing, channel split, mass-balance) at a common m, beyond U5/U6.
  - **mediator-count relations:** whether m²−1 (N1) forces a relationship with the coupling (m−1)/m or the mixing 1/(m−1) — e.g. a forced ratio of mediator count to coupling as a function of m.
  - **threshold↔constant ties:** whether the holding/criticality threshold (R7/PH5a) forces further identities beyond U4 with the gravitational or EM correspondences.
  - **cross-sector products and ratios:** further forced products/quotients in the manner of U6 (mixing × coupling = neutral channel), sought systematically across the weak observables (D11b–D11g).
  - **EM-coupling ties:** whether the forced EM coupling (the B-line, B2) enters a forced relationship with the weak mixing or the colour count at a common setting.
- **Establish:** each forced relationship as its own registered result (a new Un entry) — proof in `PROOFS_hardened.md`, confirmation in `compare.py` (forced across at least the m=2 and m=3 settings, nothing fed in), registered in `claims_physics.py` with the E tag, gate clean. Each carries a conventional cross-check **naming** the established relationship it corresponds to (§11.1 alignment, not import — the measured relationship is named, no value fed in).
- **Wall discipline (§12):** a candidate the engine does not force across fold factors is reported not-forced, plainly — never fitted. Reporting one candidate not-forced is not a stop: the next reachable composition is attempted in the same turn (§13). The step's building ends only when no further reachable composition across the listed domains remains untried (§14.5).

### Step 3 — Extend the prediction test to every forced relationship

- **State:** the test mechanism is established — T1 (forced colour count, fixed first; measured count the arbiter only) and the B2 arbiter pattern. It has not been applied to U2, U4, U5, U6, or to any relationship step 2 establishes.
- **Construct — the §11 arbiter discipline, applied to relationships:** for each forced relationship (the established U2/U4/U5/U6 and each new one from step 2), state precisely which measured comparison would confirm or falsify it, **fix the forced value first**, then bring in the measured value **only as arbiter** and report whether they coincide to a stated precision. The forced quantity is computed and recorded before the measured value is looked at; the measured value is never an input, never a fitting target (§11.2).
  - Example shape (U2): the framework forces the electroweak mixing ratio to equal the weak mass-part ratio. Fix both forced internal ratios from m; then name the measured mixing (the weak mixing angle) and the measured mass ratio as the two arbiters, and report whether the forced equality holds against them to the stated precision — the measured numbers compared, never fed in.
- **Establish:** for each relationship, a registered prediction-test entry — the forced value, the named measured arbiter, and the coincidence (or its failure) to a stated precision, run in `compare.py`, reported as the engine and the data show it. A relationship that coincides is reported confirmed; one that does not is reported failing, plainly, and is **not adjusted to fit** (§5, §9.5). Both outcomes are legitimate; neither is forced.
- **The instrument does not rule** whether any confirmed relationship is "the" novel prediction or whether it is novel at all (§9.1). Claude reports the forced value, the measured arbiter, and the match; the author and assessors judge significance and novelty.

## What completes Steps 1–3

The three steps' building ends only when no reachable construction remains untried: U3 reconfirmed on the current corpus; every forced cross-observable relationship the engine produces by composition across the domains registered (the set driven to exhaustion, not assumed closed); and each forced relationship — the established ones and every new one — put through the prediction test where a measured comparison exists, the forced value fixed first and the measured value the arbiter only. Whether the relationship set is complete, whether any forced relationship is a novel and decisive prediction, are not Claude's to declare (§9.1, §10.4) — Claude reports the engine's reading and the data's verdict; the author and assessors decide.
