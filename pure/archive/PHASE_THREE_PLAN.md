# Phase Three Plan — Unification and the Forced Prediction

*Author: Maria Smith — independent researcher. Claude provides operational assistance only: it builds, proves, gates, and reports the engine's reading; it does not decide what is established, what a result is worth, whether a wall is acceptable, or whether the framework has succeeded (GUIDANCE §1, §9, §10.4).*

## What Phase Three is

Phase Two established the four fundamental interactions in the permitted language, each with its source, force law, mediator character, and dynamics, and U1 forced every characteristic constant of all four from the single fold factor m. Phase Three has one purpose: to assemble the complete fold→physics dictionary across every established result, and to bring out the framework's **forced, testable** relationships — relationships among observables that the framework forces from the One with nothing fed in, of which U2 (the electroweak mixing ratio equals the weak mass-part ratio) is the first. A relationship counts only if it is forced by the construction, never fitted (§5, §9.5, §11).

## Governance (carried from Phase Two — GUIDANCE)

1. A result is **established** only on the Phase-One standard: built in the permitted language (one axiom; no negatives, no zero, no imaginaries; ratio and division), proven, scanned clean by the no-apparatus gate with the integrity tripwire intact, and confirmed by exact computation. A result is never entered and then claimed established while in progress (§10.1).
2. Each part is established **before** the next is begun; the sequence below is a dependency order (§10.2).
3. **No result is reached by fabrication, fitting, or relabelling (§5, §9).** The framework forces its quantities from its own axioms and engine; a measured number is never fed in as a construction input (§11.2). The single exception, defined in step 3 below, is using a measured value **only as the arbiter of an already-forced relationship** — never as an input to building it.
4. **The instrument does not adjudicate (§9).** Whether a forced relationship is the novel prediction, whether the unification is sufficient, whether a wall is acceptable, and whether Phase Three is complete are the author's and the assessors' decisions. Claude reports what the engine shows — forced / not forced, confirmed / not confirmed — and stops.
5. **No consensus wall (§2, §12).** A result is not declared unreachable because the framework does not rebuild the textbook's route to it, nor because it does not match a measured number the framework was never meant to take as input. A wall is reported only when a specific construction has been attempted in code and the engine shows a specific, named, in-language obstruction (§12.2). Before any wall, the reachable reduction is attempted in the same turn (§12, §13).
6. **The goal is the work, never the stop (§14).** No step below is a destination to reach and halt at; each is the next construction. Building ends only when no reachable construction remains untried anywhere in the programme.

## The sequence (dependency order)

### 1. The complete fold→physics dictionary
- **Builds on:** every established result (the 15 theorems, 4 observations, 63 physical correspondences, U1, U2).
- **Construct:** one explicit map from fold-structure to physical observable — for each physical quantity, the chain from the One (axiom → definition/theorem → physical correspondence) that produces it. U1 already gives this for the characteristic constants of the four forces; this step extends it to every established observable, so the unification is stated in full and not only for the constants.
- **Establish:** a single registry/document in which each physical correspondence is traced to its construction from the One, machine-checked by re-running each cited dependency. Built in the permitted language; gate-clean; confirmed by exact computation.
- **Reachable reduction first:** the dictionary is assembled from results already established, so it is reachable now; it is built by composition, not by new physics.

### 2. The full set of forced cross-observable relationships
- **Builds on:** the dictionary (step 1), and the forced quantities already in the corpus (PH5, D7b, D10g, D11b, D11g, U1, U2).
- **Construct:** every relationship the framework forces among two or more observables, each derived from the fold factor m with nothing fed in — U2 is the first instance (mixing ratio = mass-part ratio). Each candidate relationship is attempted in code: the engine either forces the equality/relation across fold factors, or it does not.
- **Establish:** each forced relationship as its own registered result (a Un entry), with its confirmation and a conventional cross-check naming the measured relationship it corresponds to (alignment, not import — §11.3).
- **Wall discipline:** if a candidate relationship is not forced (the engine does not show it across fold factors), that is reported as not-forced — the reading, not a worth-judgment (§9.4). It is never fitted into existence.

### 3. The prediction test
- **Builds on:** the forced relationships (step 2).
- **Construct:** for a forced relationship, state precisely which measured comparison would confirm or falsify it, then make that comparison. This is the one place a measured value enters: **as the arbiter of an already-forced result, never as an input to building it** (§11.2 forbids import as input; using a measured number to test a forced claim is the test the project exists to make, §11.1). The forced quantity is fixed before the measured value is looked at.
- **Establish:** the comparison run and its outcome reported as the engine and the data show it — the forced value, the measured value, and whether they coincide to a stated precision. A relationship that coincides is reported as confirmed; one that does not is reported as failing, plainly, and is **not adjusted to fit** (§5, §9.5). Both outcomes are legitimate results; neither is forced.
- **The instrument does not rule** whether a confirmed relationship is "the" novel prediction or whether it is novel at all (§9.1) — it reports the forced value, the measured value, and the match; the author and assessors judge significance and novelty.

### 4. The sequential re-walk (the verification spine)
- **Builds on:** the whole programme.
- **Construct:** re-walk the entire derivation from the One forward in dependency order — the axiom, the three definitions, the fifteen theorems, the four observations, the emergence layer, then the physical domains in dependency order (fields and the wave; relativity; quantum structure; the four forces at equal depth; the constants; the unification) — each part re-established on the Phase-One standard before the next, the gate clean and the tripwire intact throughout, `run_all.py` reporting REPRODUCTION: ALL CLEAN at every step.
- **Establish:** the single-axiom-to-unification derivation reads straight through with no part assumed and none skipped (§10.2, §14.5). This pass is the verification spine under the unification claim: the dictionary (step 1) is sound only if the whole derivation reproduces clean end to end.

### 5. A new, forced result — derived from the framework
- **Builds on:** the complete dictionary (step 1), the forced relationships (step 2), and the prediction test (step 3).
- **Construct:** a result the framework forces that is not a re-expression of an already-known fact — a quantity, relationship, or structure that the fold produces from the One and that the established physical/mathematical account does not already state in this form. It is found the same way every Phase Two result was found: by composing the framework's own constructions and reading what the engine forces, never by aiming at a target and fitting to it (§5, §9.5, §11.2). Candidate routes, attempted in code in dependency order, each kept only if forced:
  - a forced relationship among observables that the standard account leaves independent (the U-line; U2 is the first instance — others are sought by composing forced quantities across domains);
  - a forced value or ratio at a place where the standard account has a free parameter or an unexplained number, derived here from m;
  - a forced structural constraint (a count, a dimension, a selection rule, an exclusion) that follows from the fold and has an observable consequence — the way orbital stability already forced three spatial dimensions (D9f–h);
  - a forced extension or limit (a regime, a boundary case, a coincidence the framework forces) that the established treatment does not predict in this form.
- **Establish:** the result built in the permitted language, proven, gate-clean, confirmed by exact computation, and registered with its confirmation and a conventional cross-check naming the established account it extends or differs from (alignment, not import — §11.1, §11.3). Where the result implies a measured comparison, it goes through the prediction test (step 3): the forced value is fixed first, the measured value is the arbiter only, never an input.
- **Reachable reduction first (§12, §13):** before reporting that no new result is reachable, the reachable composition is attempted in the same turn — the framework's forced quantities are combined across domains and the engine is read. A wall is reported only when a specific composition has been attempted in code and the engine shows a specific in-language obstruction (§12.2); the framework is never declared incapable of a new result by holding it to a consensus standard (§2, §9).
- **The instrument does not rule on novelty (§9.1).** Claude reports what the engine forces — the value, the relationship, the construction from the One — and whether it is confirmed where a measured comparison exists. Whether the result is new, whether it is a genuine advance in science or mathematics, and whether it showcases the framework are the author's and the assessors' judgments, not Claude's. Claude does not call a result "novel," "significant," or "a showcase"; it states what was forced and what was shown, and stops (§9.2, mechanical check).
- **No fabricated novelty.** A result that turns out to re-express a known fact is reported as reproducing it (a correspondence), not dressed as new; a result the engine does not force is reported as not-forced, not asserted (§5). Neither dismissing a real new result nor manufacturing a fake one is permitted; the instrument does neither (§9.5).

## What completes Phase Three

Phase Three's building ends only when no reachable construction remains untried: the dictionary assembled, every forced cross-observable relationship the engine produces registered, each tested where a measured comparison exists, the new forced result (step 5) attempted by composition across domains and established if forced, and the sequential re-walk clean end to end. Whether the result constitutes a completed unified theory, whether any forced prediction is novel and decisive, and whether the new result is a genuine advance, are not Claude's to declare (§9.1, §10.4) — Claude reports the engine's reading and the data's verdict; the author and the assessors decide.
