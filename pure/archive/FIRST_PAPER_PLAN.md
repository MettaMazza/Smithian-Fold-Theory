## What this paper is

The first paper drawn from the theory. It does not start from scratch: the corpus already
contains a **self-observation domain** — five forced, gate-clean results, C1s through C5s — that
were established as part of the main development. This paper takes those five results as its
foundation and forces them **forward** into statements about experience: the stream, the unity,
the limits of self-knowledge, and the felt self. Every new result is forced from the One and the
fold, in the permitted language, or it is open and the work continues. No fact about minds enters
any construction; a measured or observed fact about minds may appear **only afterward**, as the
arbiter a forced result is checked against.

## The ground already in the corpus (do not re-derive — build from it)

- **C1s — self-observation is closed.** The fold *is* the act of observation: applied to a state,
  its result re-enters as part of the state. A self-observing loop never leaves the system
  (closure). A self-modelling system operating on its own states is a closed operator.
- **C2s — the blind spot.** The act of observation is two-to-one (a state and its antipode observe
  identically), so self-observation cannot recover which preimage it came from — a forced intrinsic
  limit on self-knowledge.
- **C3s — the fixed point.** Unison (the One) is the unique state unchanged by observing itself,
  fold(One) = One; the half-One observes to unison; self-coincidence below unison repels.
- **C4s — integration.** The forced holding threshold (m−1)/m (Q10) sets an integration threshold:
  below it self-observers stay separate loops; at or above it they lock into one integrated loop.
- **C5s — discrete moments.** The fold is the atomic unit act of observation (one bit each, no
  partial fold), so observation proceeds in discrete, indivisible steps — quantised moments.

## The honest standing of the arbiters (stated once, not as a flinch)

The matter and cosmological sectors were checked against hard arbiters — measured masses, ratios,
mixing entries — to a few parts in a hundred thousand. The arbiters here are softer: the
psychophysics of perception, the phenomenology of unity and self, the empirical character of
introspection. So "confirmed against measurement" is a weaker claim in this sector than in the
matter sector, and the paper states that plainly for each result rather than letting it be found.
This is not a reason to stop; it is a reason to be exact about what each result claims and against
what it is checked.

## The plan — phases in dependency order

Each phase: build forward in the permitted language; gate-clean; register; state the arbiter and
what the result does and does not claim; report to Maria; her ruling before the next phase.

### Phase 1 — Name the One (the whole of the sector)
Before any new result. Fix what the One *is* for self-observation — the whole of which a moment of
experience is a part. The corpus points at one candidate: the self-observing loop itself (C1s) is
the One, and a single act of observation (one fold, C5s) is a part of it. Settle this. Nothing in
Phases 2–5 is legitimate until the author rules the One of this sector.
**Rests on:** C1s, C5s. **Exit:** the author's ruling on the One.

### Phase 2 — The stream from the moments
C5s forces discrete, atomic moments. Force how a sequence of single folds composes into the felt
continuity of a stream of experience, and force whatever the structure forces about its grain —
whether there is a minimal moment, a forced cadence, a "frame" of experience. Report the forced
structure; do not assert a rate the construction does not force.
**Rests on:** C5s, Phase 1. **Arbiter:** the psychophysics of perceptual moments / discrete
sampling in perception. **What it must not claim:** a specific millisecond rate unless forced.

### Phase 3 — The unity of experience
C4s forces an integration threshold at (m−1)/m (Q10). Force why experience is unified rather than
fragmented — the binding of many self-observing parts into one loop — and force what the threshold
says about where unity holds and where it breaks.
**Rests on:** C4s, Q10, Phase 1. **Arbiter:** the all-or-nothing character of conscious access;
binding; the split/unified boundary (e.g. split-brain phenomena, as arbiter only).

### Phase 4 — The limit of self-knowledge (the blind spot)
C2s forces a two-to-one blind spot in self-observation. Force what a self-model structurally cannot
know about itself, and force whether that limit has a definite shape (which distinctions collapse).
**Rests on:** C2s, Phase 1. **Arbiter:** the empirical unreliability of introspection; the forced
existence of a self-model's blind spot. **What it must not claim:** a specific psychological
illusion unless the collapse maps to it by construction.

### Phase 5 — The fixed point and the felt self
C3s forces unison as the unique self-observation fixed point — the one state that returns itself
when observed. Force whether the stable "sense of being a self" is that fixed point: the state a
self-observing loop settles to, the one thing observation leaves unchanged. This is the boldest
phase and the one nearest "why there is experience at all." It is forced forward like the rest, or
it is open; it is not asserted.
**Rests on:** C3s, C1s, Phase 1, and Phases 2–4 as available. **Arbiter:** the phenomenology of a
persistent self; the felt invariant through changing experience.
